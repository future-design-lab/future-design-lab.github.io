New Space Ritual Objects (Real Artefacts with Symbolic or Ritual Significance in New Space). This corpus gathers 40 space objects of ritual and cultural-symbolic significance from around the world, spanning the iconic heritage of spacefaring powers such as the United States, China, Europe, and Japan. From the Voyager Golden Record of 1977 to the 2022 UN-NASA space concert, these objects transcend mere technological-tool status to become material carriers of humanity's exploratory spirit, cultural confidence, and emotional attachment.

The corpus interprets each object's historical background, symbolic meaning, and cultural value from multiple perspectives, fusing art, the humanities, society, and technology. It suits space-humanities research, art-exhibition curation, interdisciplinary teaching, and academic writing, offering a systematic and complete reference for understanding the cultural dimension of contemporary space exploration.

### **RS-01｜The Voyager Golden Record**

Description: In 1977, NASA launched the copper "Golden Record" aboard Voyager 1 and Voyager 2, encoding Earth's languages, music, natural sounds, and images as humanity's greeting to the cosmos. The object embodies a wish for cross-cultural, cross-planetary communication and has become a classic ritual object in the history of space communication. Its gold-plated coating ensures information integrity over billions of years in the extreme space environment. It contains greetings in 55 languages, 90 minutes of music (Bach, Mozart, percussion, jazz, and more), natural sounds (thunder, wind, birdsong), and 116 scientific and humanistic images.

Source: Sagan, C., Druyan, A., Ferris, T., Lomberg, J., & Sagan, L. (1978). Murmurs of Earth: The Voyager Interstellar Record. Random House. https://voyager.jpl.nasa.gov/golden-record/

Keywords: interstellar communication, cultural symbol, humanity's collective memory, ritual across time and space

### **RS-02｜Apollo 11 Lunar Flag**

Description: In 1969, on humanity's first Moon landing, Armstrong and Aldrin planted a U.S. flag on the lunar surface. The act was not only a national symbol but a commemorative gesture of civilization expanding its frontier. The flag became a collective symbol of space politics, ritual behavior, and visual narrative. Though faded in the Moon's extreme environment, its symbolic meaning as a "human footprint" never fades, representing sovereignty assertion, cultural expansion, and the concentration of an exploratory spirit. During Apollo 11, the flag-planting ceremony was carefully designed and broadcast globally, becoming one of the most ritualistic moments in human history.

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_Flag_Assembly

Keywords: political symbol, landing ritual, visual narrative, national identity, territorial claim

### **RS-03｜Wedding Rings Flown to ISS**

Description: In 2003, NASA astronaut Yury Malenchenko held a wedding via remote ground video from the International Space Station, having deliberately sent his ring and attire up aboard a cargo ship in advance. The space wedding and its objects were endowed with a function of emotional continuity through "ritual transfer." This innovative wedding broke the boundary between Earth and space, elevating a private love ritual into an interstellar cultural event. The wedding witnessed in orbit symbolizes the eternal presence of human emotion in the cosmos.

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuri_Malenchenko

Keywords: marriage symbol, private ritual, space emotional continuity, love across boundaries

### **RS-04｜Apollo Lunar Communion Kit**

Description: Apollo 11 crew member Buzz Aldrin privately held a communion ceremony inside the lunar module, using a small chalice and wine. Though never publicly displayed, this religious object's privacy and symbolism reinforced the faith dimension of behavior in space. The moment fused spiritual belief with scientific exploration, reflecting humanity's deep need to seek spiritual solace even in the most alien environment. Aldrin later recalled that performing a religious ceremony on the lunar surface represented awe of a higher power and adherence to personal faith.

Source: Aldrin, B. (2009). Magnificent Desolation: The Long Journey Home from the Moon. Harmony Books. https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6351887-magnificent-desolation

The coin's presence on the Martian surface became visual evidence broadcast by global media, turning a scientific achievement into a cultural symbol. The coin represents humanity's formal "naming" and "claiming" of Mars — a classic example of space objects in a neo-colonial age.

### **RS-08｜Mission Patches**

Description: Since the Apollo program, NASA has designed a dedicated patch for each crewed flight, worn on astronauts' garments. These patches use image-encoding to reinforce mission identity, team belonging, and collective memory, with strong symbolic meaning. Each patch is a unique artwork fusing mission goals, team name, and symbolic imagery. After being worn in space, patches return to Earth with the astronauts as physical witnesses to the mission. Widely collected and displayed, they have become visual cultural symbols of the space age.

Source: NASA. (2023). Crew Mission Patches Collection. https://www.nasa.gov/gallery/human-spaceflight-mission-patches/

Keywords: identity symbol, mission culture, visual inscription, collective identity

### **RS-09｜Seeds Flown to Space**

Description: Beginning with Apollo 14 in 1971, the U.S. Department of Agriculture sent tree seeds into orbit, later planting them on Earth as "Moon Trees." Thousands now grow worldwide. These seeds/plants are imbued with the meaning of a "cosmic journey," symbolizing the continuation and sowing of Earth's life. The "Moon Tree" project links space exploration with ecological protection, reflecting humanity's care for Earth's life. As living monuments, the trees witness the history of human space exploration while symbolizing the resilience and continuity of Earth's life in the cosmos.

Source: NASA. (2024). Learn About The First Moon Trees. https://www.nasa.gov/stem-content/learn-about-the-first-moon-trees/

### **RS-10｜Fallen Astronaut Memorial**

Description: In 1971, Apollo 15 astronaut David Scott quietly placed a small aluminum figurine and a plaque listing fallen astronauts on the lunar surface — the only publicly acknowledged human-monument space object, embodying an act of "remembrance" in space. The small figurine represents all fallen astronauts and became the most symbolic memorial on the Moon. The secret act was later made public, sparking deep discussion of space rules and humanitarian spirit.

Source: Scott, D., & Wagener, L. (2004). Two Sides of the Moon. Thomas Dunne Books.

Keywords: death ritual, remembrance mechanism, memorial installation, eternal commemoration
